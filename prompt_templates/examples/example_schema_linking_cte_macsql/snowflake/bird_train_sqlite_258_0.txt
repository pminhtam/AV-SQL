To compute this, complaint records must be filtered by client location (city = 'New York') and by year of the complaint date (2016 and 2017).
The retail.client table provides column "city" to identify New York clients and column "client_id",  to join with a complaints/logs table (not shown in the snippet).
"district_id" is a foreign key in "client" that links to retail.district, which in turn links to state and division, but since the question focuses on city and years, we only need to filter by "city" in "client".
This makes retail.client relevant.
The "retail.district" and "retail.state" tables are not strictly required for this computation because the external knowledge and question focus on city and years, not on district or state aggregation, so they can be dropped.
